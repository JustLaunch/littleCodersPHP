Kids can learn to code visually

In most schools children are not being taught computer science until they reach high school, if at all. Only 0.7% of Advanced Placement High School courses are computer science. This means unless children or parents take it upon themselves, most students don't get any training in computer science until college.

It's little wonder that only 2.4% of college students graduate with a degree in computer science. littleCoders changes this. Using smart phones and tablets, kids spend hours every day playing games. littleCoders turns these games into a productive, fun, and engaging experience by teaching children skills that are some of the most sought after and useful in both today's and future generations. This novel approach gives children access to learn how to code during their earliest and most impressionable ages - 8 years old and younger.
